A Week at Atwea: Seeking God on the Sacred Heights

FOX 7 Austin

10 April 2024

FOX 7 Austin . 25 April 2024

it was a cool Monday morning when a group of thirty believers arrived at the base of the revered Atwea Mountain, a serene retreat spot known across Ghana as a place of spiritual renewal. They came with hearts ready to seek God through fasting, prayer, and the study of His Word. The journey had been long, but their spirits were high as they disembarked from the bus, unloading tents, mats, and their personal items. The campsite was a simple clearing surrounded by the lush greenery of the mountain. The air was filled with the sound of rustling leaves and distant bird calls. Under the guidance of Pastor Daniel, the group quickly set up their tents. Each believer had brought a personal Bible, a notebook, and a heart expectant for the week ahead.

By late afternoon, the group gathered for their first session under a large open canopy. Pastor Daniel welcomed everyone. “We’ve come to Atwea not for extraordinary signs, but for the ordinary grace that God gives through prayer, fasting, and His Word,” he said. “Some of us will fast entirely, others partially. Some may eat simple meals. What matters is that our hearts remain focused on Him.”

The Meal Plan and Daily Rhythm

Each day followed a structured schedule. Mornings began at 5:00 AM with a time of personal devotion, as individuals found quiet corners on the mountain to read their Bibles and pray. By 7:00 AM, the group gathered for corporate prayer and worship. The sound of their voices filled the air, blending harmoniously with the natural surroundings. Breakfast was simple, reflecting the spirit of fasting. Those who were fasting entirely abstained, sipping only water. Others who needed light sustenance for health reasons ate modest portions of porridge made from millet or corn, sometimes with a small piece of bread. “It’s not about the food,” Sister Esi explained, “but about keeping our bodies aligned with our spirits in seeking God.” The afternoons were marked by teaching sessions led by Pastor Daniel and other group leaders. Topics ranged from “The Power of Prayer” to “Walking in Obedience.” After each teaching, there were breakout discussions where believers shared personal insights and reflections. Lunch, for those who ate, was equally modest: steamed plantains or boiled yam with a light vegetable stew. For dinner, they prepared something warm and easy to digest, like rice water or soup, but only for those who were not fasting. Many chose to forego meals entirely during the day, dedicating their time to prayer and Scripture.

Prayer, Gospel Teaching, and Reflection

The evenings were the highlight of each day. The group gathered under the stars for a time of worship and intercession. Pastor Daniel often preached a short message, grounding the group in Scripture. On the third night, his message was on the theme of perseverance in prayer. “Just as Jesus often withdrew to pray, we too must make space in our lives to seek the Father earnestly,” he said. Throughout the week, the believers experienced the simplicity and beauty of consistent prayer. There were no dramatic signs or wonders, only the steady assurance of God’s presence. Sister Ama, who had struggled with anxiety for years, shared during one session, “I haven’t heard an audible voice or seen visions, but I feel peace like I’ve never known before.” Brother Kwame, who was fasting partially, reflected on the teachings. “This week has reminded me that the gospel is not just about receiving blessings but about living in daily surrender to Christ,” he said.

The Final Day: A Call to Action

By the seventh day, the group felt both physically stretched and spiritually rejuvenated. As the sun rose, Pastor Daniel led them in a final session on the summit of Atwea Mountain. With their hands lifted high, they prayed for their families, communities, and the nation. The simplicity of the moment was its power—a group of ordinary believers, united in their pursuit of God. As they packed their tents and prepared to leave, Pastor Daniel encouraged them. “What we have experienced here is not meant to stay on this mountain. Let it flow into your daily lives. Keep praying, keep fasting, keep seeking Him.” As the bus descended from the mountain, each person carried a renewed sense of purpose. There had been no extraordinary events, but the week had been marked by profound spiritual growth. Atwea Mountain had become a place of transformation—not through dramatic encounters, but through the quiet, steady work of God in the hearts of His people.
